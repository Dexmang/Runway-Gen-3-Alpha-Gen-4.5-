This Vantage left the factory with a base P.O.E. price of $191,000. The original owner then specified $21,600 in factory options, paid $6,700 in import costs and $3,500 in transportation and handling. The total window sticker reads **$222,800**.

The $5,800 forged wheels alone cost more than most car payments. The AMR Line Package, cooled seats, smoked tail lamps, yellow calipers -- this is the spec sheet of someone who knew exactly what they wanted. A buyer ordering this configuration today would wait months and pay full sticker. This one is ready now.

### The Price

**$199,007.** That is $23,793 below the original window sticker -- a 10.7% discount on a car that is, by any reasonable measure, new.

### Why Below Sticker?

Buyers will ask. The answer is simple and clean:

- **One owner.** Clean title. No accidents. No stories.
- **~2,519 miles.** That is a weekend car with a few tanks of gas through it. The break-in period barely ended.
- **Factory warranty transfers.** Three years, unlimited mileage, to the next owner. Plus 24-hour roadside assistance. The new buyer gets the full factory safety net.
- **Private sale.** No dealer markup, no doc fees, no reconditioning charges. The savings pass directly to the buyer.

The car is not priced below sticker because something is wrong. It is priced below sticker because a private seller does not carry the overhead of a dealership -- and this buyer gets the benefit.

## 3. 007 Messaging Framework

### Philosophy

The 007 connection is baked into Aston Martin's DNA. We do not need to force it. The price -- $199,007 -- does the heavy lifting. Everything else is a nod, not a nudge. The goal is for the buyer to notice it, smile, and feel like they are in on something. Never hit them over the head with it.

### Layered References

1. **The price itself.** $199,007. This is the primary hook. It stops the scroll. It makes people do a double-take. It gets shared.
2. **The garage spot.** The car sits in spot 007. This shows up naturally in the video content. Do not add a graphic overlay or call it out in text -- let the viewer catch it.
3. **The video clip.** One of the walkaround clips is titled "007." This is an easter egg for anyone browsing the media gallery. It rewards attention.
4. **The heritage.** Aston Martin has appeared in more Bond films than any other brand. The Vantage lineage is part of that story. A single, confident sentence is enough.

### Headline & Tagline Options

1. **"$199,007."**
   Use the price as a standalone headline. No explanation needed. The number is the hook.

2. **"The name is Vantage. 2026 Vantage."**
   One-time use only. Hero section or social launch post. Does not need repeating.

3. **"License to Drive."**
   Works for a subheadline under the hero image or as a gallery section header. Light, earned, not forced.

4. **"656 Reasons. One Number."**
   Bridges the performance spec to the price. Works for a specs section header or paid social.

5. **"Spot 007. Asking $199,007. Coincidence is for other brands."**
   This is for social media captions or the "about this listing" copy. Conversational tone. Winks at the connection without explaining the joke.

### What to Avoid

- Do not use "Bond" or "James Bond" in listing titles or headlines. Trademark territory, and it cheapens the reference.
- Do not use gun barrel graphics, tuxedo imagery, or movie stills. This is an Aston Martin, not a movie prop.
- Do not over-explain the 007 angle. If it needs explaining, you have already lost the moment.

## 4. Section-by-Section Copy Direction

### Hero Section

- **Headline:** "$199,007."
- **Subheadline:** "2026 Aston Martin Vantage Coupe. One owner. 2,519 miles. Full factory warranty."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - Lead with the price as the disruptor. A sub-$200K Vantage with this spec does not exist at any dealer.
  - Immediately establish the condition: one owner, negligible miles, warranty intact.
  - The hero image should be the car at its most dramatic -- three-quarter front, low angle, Jet Black paint catching light.
- **Tone:** Commanding. Confident. Let the car and the number do the talking. Minimal words, maximum impact.

### Value / Options Section

- **Headline:** "$21,600 in Factory Options. $23,793 Below Sticker."
- **Subheadline:** "Specified right. Priced right."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - Walk through each option with its factory cost. Let the buyer add up the value themselves.
  - Emphasize that this is the exact specification an enthusiast would build -- blacked out, AMR-packaged, forged wheels, cooled seats.
  - Close with the total MSRP vs. asking price comparison table.
- **Tone:** Factual, detailed, satisfying. This is where the analytical buyer gets convinced. Present the numbers cleanly and let them land.

### Gallery Section

- **Headline:** "Every Angle. Nothing to Hide."
- **Subheadline:** "License to drive."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - Gallery speaks for itself. The copy should be minimal here.
  - If captions are used, keep them factual: "21-inch Y-Spoke forged wheels, Satin Black." "Smoked red tail lamps." "Onyx Black haircalf leather with AMR Lime accents."
  - The gallery must include the 007 garage spot visible naturally in at least one frame.
- **Tone:** Clean, editorial. Let the photography carry the weight. Copy is a whisper, not a shout.

### Interior Section

- **Headline:** "Onyx. Haircalf. AMR Lime."
- **Subheadline:** "The specification a configurator cannot prepare you for."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - Haircalf leather is not standard on any competitor at this price. It is a tactile, visual differentiator.
  - The AMR Lime accents against Onyx Black create a cockpit that looks and feels like a purpose-built machine.
  - Call out the cooled front seats, heated steering wheel, 12-way memory seats -- this is a grand touring interior in a sports car body.
- **Tone:** Sensory. Descriptive. Help the buyer feel the materials before they sit in the car.

### Specs / Performance Section

- **Headline:** "656 HP. 590 lb-ft. Rear-Mid Engine."
- **Subheadline:** "The most powerful production Vantage ever built."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - This is the completely redesigned Vantage -- 155 horsepower more than the outgoing model. That is not a refresh. It is a new car.
  - Rear-mid mounted 8-speed automatic, electronic rear differential with dynamic torque vectoring, carbon fiber prop shaft. The engineering is serious.
  - Five drive modes including Track and Wet. This car was designed to be driven, not parked.
- **Tone:** Technical but accessible. Enthusiast-grade detail without engineering jargon. The buyer should feel the competence of the machine.

### Contact / Inquiry Section

- **Headline:** "Serious Inquiries."
- **Subheadline:** "Private sale. Chicagoland, IL. Clean title. Warranty transfers to new owner."
- **Key Messaging:**
  - Reiterate the key trust signals: one owner, clean title, factory warranty, private sale.
  - Location: Chicagoland, IL. Available for in-person inspection.
  - Financing and transport arrangements can be discussed. This is a white-glove transaction.
- **Tone:** Direct, professional, confident. No desperation. This is a car that will sell -- the question is to whom.

## 5. Target Buyer Profile

### Primary Buyer

- **Age:** 35-55
- **Household income:** $350K-$750K+
- **Net worth:** $2M+
- **Current garage:** Porsche 911 (991.2 or 992), Mercedes-AMG GT, BMW M8, Jaguar F-Type R, or a previous-generation Vantage. Possibly a daily SUV (Cayenne, X5M, GLS) alongside a weekend car.
- **Occupation:** Business owner, executive, medical professional, tech lead, or finance. Someone who earns well but still pays attention to value.

### What Motivates Them

- **The deal.** This buyer is not cheap -- they are strategic. They know what a new Vantage costs at a dealer and they know they cannot get this spec for under $220K anywhere else. Saving $23,793 on a car they were already shopping for is not a compromise -- it is a win.
- **The spec.** They have been on the Aston Martin configurator. They know the AMR package. They know what Satin Black forged wheels cost. This car is already built the way they would build it.
- **The story.** $199,007. Garage spot 007. They will tell this story at every dinner and every cars-and-coffee. The car comes with a built-in conversation piece.
- **The upgrade.** They want to move up from a Porsche or AMG into something rarer. The Vantage occupies a different lane -- fewer on the road, more presence, unmistakable sound.

### Objections and How to Close Them

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Why is it priced below sticker?" | One owner, private sale, no dealer overhead. Full warranty transfers. The car is essentially new -- the savings are real, not a red flag. |
| "I would rather buy new." | You will wait 3-6 months for allocation, pay full MSRP or above, and still need to spec the options. This car is here now, specced perfectly, and $23,793 less. |
| "Is something wrong with it?" | Clean title. 2,519 miles. Factory warranty through 2029. 24-hour roadside. If there were a problem, the warranty would catch it. |
| "I am not sure about buying private." | This is a straightforward, transparent transaction. All documentation provided. Inspection welcome. Warranty transfers to you directly from Aston Martin. |
| "That is still a lot of money." | It is. And it is $23,793 less than it should be for a car with 155 more horsepower than the one it replaced, a full factory warranty, and a spec that would cost $222,800 to replicate. |

### Secondary Buyer

- **The collector.** Already owns exotics. Sees the Vantage as a portfolio addition. Motivated by the new-generation platform and the AMR spec. Less price-sensitive but still appreciates buying smart.
- **The Bond fan with means.** This buyer exists. The 007 price is not a gimmick to them -- it is the reason they click. They want the story as much as the car.

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### vs. Buying New from a Dealer

| Factor | New (Dealer) | This Listing |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Price | $222,800+ (MSRP, likely higher with dealer markup) | $199,007 |
| Wait time | 3-6 months for allocation and delivery | Available now |
| Negotiation | Minimal to none on a new-gen Aston | Price is published, transparent |
| Spec control | Must order and wait, or settle for dealer stock | Already built to an elite specification |
| Warranty | 3 years from delivery | 3 years from original delivery (nearly full remaining) |
| Dealer fees | Doc fees, prep fees, potential ADM | Zero. Private sale. |

### vs. Competitors at This Price

- **Porsche 911 Turbo (~$195K-$215K):** More common, less exclusive. The Vantage turns heads the 911 cannot. Different league of presence.
- **Mercedes-AMG GT (~$175K-$210K):** Competent but outgoing platform. The Vantage is the newer, more advanced car.
- **Maserati MC20 (~$215K+):** Less proven ownership experience, thinner dealer network, lower residuals.
- **McLaren Artura (~$225K+):** Hybrid complexity, polarizing reliability reputation, limited dealer network.

The Vantage at $199,007 sits in a space no competitor can match: new-generation platform, factory warranty, sub-$200K price, and a brand with 110 years of heritage backing every mile.
